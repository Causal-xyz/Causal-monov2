01 What is Futarchy

Futarchy is a governance model proposed by economist Robin Hanson where decisions are made by markets, not votes. Instead of asking "Do you support this proposal?", futarchy asks "What does the market think will happen if this proposal passes?"

The core idea: Vote on values, bet on beliefs. A proposal's outcome (Yes or No) is determined by comparing the market prices of conditional tokens. If "Yes" conditional tokens trade at a higher price, the market believes the proposal is beneficial — and the proposal passes.

05 Phase 2 — AMM Setup
PHASE 2 Create Uniswap V3 Pools Proposal Owner Only

The owner calls createAndSetAmms() to create two Uniswap V3 pools and provide full-range liquidity. This can only be called once. The owner must first split tokens so the contract holds enough yesX, noX, and USDC.

YES Pool
yesX / yesUsdc
Prices tokenX in the "proposal passes" world. Higher price = market believes proposal is good for tokenX.
NO Pool
noX / noUsdc
Prices tokenX in the "proposal fails" world. Higher price = market believes tokenX is better without the proposal.
Full-Range Liquidity: Uses tick range [-887220, 887220] (max possible, rounded to tick spacing). This is equivalent to Uniswap V2 behavior — liquidity across the entire price range. The initial price is set via sqrtPriceX96 parameter.
06 Phase 3 & 5 — Split / Merge
SPLIT Enter the Market Anyone (with approval)
splitX(amount, receiver) Deposit tokenX, receive yes+no pairs
100 tokenX → contract locks → 100 yesX + 100 noX
Value-neutral: 100 tokenX = 100 yesX + 100 noX. If YES wins, 100 yesX redeems for 100 tokenX. If NO wins, 100 noX redeems for 100 tokenX. Either way exactly 100 tokenX is distributed.
splitUsdc(amount, receiver) Deposit USDC, receive yes+no pairs
1000 USDC → contract locks → 1000 yesUsdc + 1000 noUsdc
reverse operation
MERGE Exit the Market Anyone (with balance)
mergeX(amount, receiver) Return matched pairs, get back underlying
100 yesX + 100 noX → burn both → 100 tokenX
Merging is risk-free exit — always returns exact underlying regardless of market conditions. Also enables arbitrage: if yesX + noX prices diverge from tokenX price, split/merge to profit.
07 Phase 4 — Trading Period
TRADE Price Discovery on Uniswap V3 Anyone (on-chain)

Trading happens externally on Uniswap V3 pools, not within the contract. After splitting, users hold both yes and no tokens. They trade based on their beliefs:

Bullish (Believes YES)
1. Split tokenX → get yesX + noX
2. Sell noX on NO pool
3. Or buy more yesX on YES pool

Drives yesX price UP
Bearish (Believes NO)
1. Split tokenX → get yesX + noX
2. Sell yesX on YES pool
3. Or buy more noX on NO pool

Drives noX price UP
Key insight: The markets are asking "What will tokenX be worth if this proposal passes vs. if it fails?" The relative prices encode the market's collective intelligence about the proposal's impact.

Tie-breaking: If TWAPs are exactly equal, NO wins (the > comparison means equal defaults to No). This favors the status quo.

Execution: If YES wins, the resolve() function automatically calls transferToken.safeTransfer(recipient, transferAmount) — the governance action takes effect immediately.

09 TWAP Oracle Mechanism

TWAP (Time-Weighted Average Price) is the average price over a time window, weighted by duration. This makes it resistant to flash loan attacks and momentary price manipulation.

How getTwap() works:

// Query Uniswap V3 oracle at two points:
secondsAgos = [twapWindow, 0] // twapWindow seconds ago, now
(tickCumulatives, _) = pool.observe(secondsAgos)
// Average tick over the twapWindow:
avgTick = (tickCumulatives[1] - tickCumulatives[0]) / twapWindow
// Higher tick = higher price (price = 1.0001^tick)
5 min TWAP
Low manipulation resistance. Very fresh prices. Easily attackable.
1 hr TWAP (default)
Good balance of manipulation resistance and price freshness. Quadratic capital cost for attacks.
24 hr TWAP
Very high resistance but prices may be stale and lag real market conditions.
10 Phase 7 & 8 — Redemption & Execution
REDEEM Winners Claim Underlying Assets Winning token holders
If YES Wins
yesX → burn → tokenX
yesUsdc → burn → USDC
noX and noUsdc become worthless.
If NO Wins
noX → burn → tokenX
noUsdc → burn → USDC
yesX and yesUsdc become worthless. No execution happens.
Conservation law: The math always balances. Total tokenX in contract = Total yesX outstanding = Total noX outstanding. Whether YES or NO wins, exactly the right amount of underlying is available for redemption.
